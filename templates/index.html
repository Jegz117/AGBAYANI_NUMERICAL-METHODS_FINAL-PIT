Core Formula

\[P_n(x)=\sum_{k=0}^{n} y_kL_k(x)\] \[L_k(x)=\prod_{\substack{j=0\\j\ne k}}^{n}\frac{x-x_j}{x_k-x_j}\]

Use this method for equal or unequal spacing, provided all x-values are distinct.

Mathematical Discussion

What is Lagrange Interpolation?

Lagrange interpolation constructs a unique polynomial of degree at most n that passes through n + 1 data points. It is commonly used when x-values are not equally spaced because it does not require forward, backward, or central difference tables.

Any spacing

The x-values may be equally or unequally spaced, as long as every x-value is distinct.

Basis logic

Each basis polynomial becomes 1 at its own point and 0 at all other data points.

Best for small data sets

The method is clear and effective for small n, but high-degree curves may oscillate.

Two Fully Worked Examples

Example 1

Estimate y at x = 1.5

Given: (0, 2), (1, 3), (2, 6)

Formula

\[P_2(x)=y_0L_0(x)+y_1L_1(x)+y_2L_2(x)\]

Substitute and evaluate the basis polynomials

\[L_0(1.5)=\frac{(1.5-1)(1.5-2)}{(0-1)(0-2)}=\frac{-0.25}{2}=-0.1250\]

\[L_1(1.5)=\frac{(1.5-0)(1.5-2)}{(1-0)(1-2)}=\frac{-0.75}{-1}=0.7500\]

\[L_2(1.5)=\frac{(1.5-0)(1.5-1)}{(2-0)(2-1)}=\frac{0.75}{2}=0.3750\]

Final substitution

\[P_2(1.5)=2(-0.1250)+3(0.7500)+6(0.3750)\]

Final answer

\[\boxed{P_2(1.5)=4.2500}\]

Example 2

Estimate y at x = 1

Given: (-1, 4), (0, 1), (2, 3), (3, 10)

Formula

\[P_3(x)=\sum_{k=0}^{3}y_kL_k(x)\]

Substitute and evaluate the basis polynomials

\[L_0(1)=\frac{(1-0)(1-2)(1-3)}{(-1-0)(-1-2)(-1-3)}=\frac{2}{-12}=-0.1667\]

\[L_1(1)=\frac{(1-(-1))(1-2)(1-3)}{(0-(-1))(0-2)(0-3)}=\frac{4}{6}=0.6667\]

\[L_2(1)=\frac{(1-(-1))(1-0)(1-3)}{(2-(-1))(2-0)(2-3)}=\frac{-4}{-6}=0.6667\]

\[L_3(1)=\frac{(1-(-1))(1-0)(1-2)}{(3-(-1))(3-0)(3-2)}=\frac{-2}{12}=-0.1667\]

Final substitution

\[P_3(1)=4(-0.1667)+1(0.6667)+3(0.6667)+10(-0.1667)\]

Final answer

\[\boxed{P_3(1)=0.3333}\]
